Hormone Theories: Estrogen: Helps keep tissue tight [9, 10]. Progesterone: Makes tissue lax [10-12]. Testosterone: Libido, hair; doesn't replace estrogen tightness [13, 14].
  • Estrogen: Helps keep tissue **tight**. [54, citing 9, 10]
    • Lack of estrogen → laxity (e.g., menopause). Estrogen → tight/perky tissues (skin, face). [52, citing 7, 8]
  • Progesterone: Makes tissue **lax**. [54, citing 10-12]
    • Presence of progesterone → laxity (e.g., pregnancy, pubic symphysis relaxes). [52, citing 8]
    • Estrogen & progesterone: opposite effects on tissue tightness. [52, citing 1]
  • Testosterone: Libido, hair; **doesn't replace estrogen tightness**. [54, citing 13, 14]
    • Known effects: helps libido, potential head hair growth. [52, citing 16]
    • Limitations: hair growth elsewhere (chin, body), uncontrollable distribution. Crucially, **doesn't replace estrogen's tightening role**. [52, citing 15-17]
    • Potential insights from testosterone lab results for your situation? [2]
